# 🧠 Learning Paradigms in Machine Learning

## 🤔 What are Learning Paradigms?
Learning paradigms are the **different ways** a machine
can learn from data. Just like humans learn in different
ways, machines also have 3 main ways to learn.

---

## 🌍 Real Life Example

> 🏫 **School Scenario:**
> - Supervised = Teacher gives you questions AND answers
> - Unsupervised = Teacher gives questions, you figure out patterns
> - Reinforcement = You learn by playing a game, win or lose

---

## 🔑 3 Types of Learning

### 1. Supervised Learning
- You give data WITH labels (correct answers)
- Machine learns from examples
- Predicts on new unseen data
- Example: Spam detection, Disease diagnosis

### 2. Unsupervised Learning
- You give data WITHOUT labels
- Machine finds hidden patterns on its own
- No right or wrong answer
- Example: Customer grouping, Anomaly detection

### 3. Reinforcement Learning
- Machine learns by trial and error
- Gets reward for good actions, penalty for bad
- Keeps improving to maximize reward
- Example: Self driving cars, Game playing AI

---

## 📌 Key Terms

| Term | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| Label | Correct answer | Spam / Not Spam |
| Reward | Positive feedback | +1 point for right move |
| Penalty | Negative feedback | -1 point for wrong move |
| Pattern | Hidden structure in data | Customers who buy similar things |
| Agent | The ML model in RL | Self driving car |

---

## 💡 Why It Matters
- Choosing the wrong paradigm wastes time
- Most real world problems use Supervised Learning
- Unsupervised is used when labels are expensive to get
- Reinforcement is used in games and robotics
